Settlers Of Asgard

Forge Your Saga in the Realm of Gods

Build, strategize, and conquer in Settlers Of Asgard, a multiplayer game inspired by Norse mythology. Gather resources, construct powerful structures, invoke ancient gods, and outwit your rivals to claim victory.

Discover the Story

The Saga Begins

In the mythical realms touched by Yggdrasil, aspiring chieftains rise to claim their place among legends. As a settler in the lands of Asgard, you must harness the power of runes, build formidable settlements, and seek the favor of the gods themselves. Navigate treacherous landscapes, manage resources wisely, and engage in strategic battles against rivals vying for dominance. Will you build a legacy worthy of the sagas, or fade into oblivion?

Key Features

Build Your Settlement

Acquire land and construct vital structures like Runetables and Surtur's Smeltery to expand your influence.

Invoke the Gods

Pledge allegiance to powerful Norse deities like Freyr or Loki. Build statues and utilize their unique blessings or deals.

Discover Artifacts

Unearth potent artifacts like Mjölnir Charms or Odin's Eye hidden across the land to gain strategic advantages.

Multiplayer Strategy

Compete against other players in engaging, turn-based gameplay. Outsmart your opponents to achieve victory.

Unique Art Style

Experience a visually distinct world brought to life with original artwork and card designs created by our team's artist.

Explore Norse Mythology

Immerse yourself in a world inspired by ancient myths, featuring legendary gods, artifacts, and realms from Norse lore.

Explore the Realms

Navigate the diverse landscapes of Asgard, from fiery Muspelheim to icy Niflheim. Discover pre-placed structures offering unique advantages and challenges across the interconnected realms. Strategic expansion is key!

How to Run


# To start the Server:
java -jar SettlersOfAsgard.jar server <listenport>

# To start the Client:
java -jar SettlersOfAsgard.jar client <serverip>:<serverport> [username]

Replace `<listenport>`, `<serverip>`, `<serverport>`, and `[username]` with your desired values.
